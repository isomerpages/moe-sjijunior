<p>Dear SMS / SJIJ Old Boys
<br>
<br>As broadcast below, we would like to share more details about the fund
raising (for the first time!) so that you can appreciate the efforts on
why we are doing this.
<br>
<br>1.&nbsp; Through the generosity of the Class of ’66 (no kidding!) some
years ago, a few Old Boys got together and decided to create a fund (the
St Michael’s School Alumni Fund is under the control of the SJIJ and not
by SMSAA) to assist needy students.&nbsp; The Fund is not a separate registered
association but merely funds collected and disbursed and this is administered
by SJIJ with the recommendation of the committee created by the Class of
‘66.
<br>
<br>2.&nbsp; The Fund will support needy students (on various financial schemes)
in areas where they don’t get MOE support.
<br>
<br>3.&nbsp; On average, there are about 40 needy students every year.
<br>
<br>4.&nbsp; Annual disbursements from the Fund are about $15k.
<br>
<br>5.&nbsp; A Hong Bao Festival has been held every year for these needy
students where they are given a bursary of sorts and some gifts.
<br>
<br>6.&nbsp; The Car Wash is meant to inculcate our SMS / SJI values of giving.
<br>
<br>7.&nbsp; The Fund can only continue with the generosity of the Old Boys
of SMS / SJIJ.
<br>
<br>We, the Old Boys of SMS / SJIJ, have been privileged to have enjoyed our
time at SMS / SJIJ.&nbsp; We would have met some of these needy students
in our time.&nbsp; In fact, some of us may have been the beneficiaries
of the Fund.&nbsp; So, now, we ask you to step up and be counted, with
your generosity in the following areas: -
<br>
<br>I.&nbsp; Sponsors – in any form (e.g. gift packs)
<br>
<br>II.&nbsp; Donations – in any amounts (if you contribute at least $200,
you get a tax deduction receipt – a big incentive!!!)&nbsp;<strong>AND</strong>&nbsp;if
you could, bring your car back to SJIJ to be washed
<br>
<br>III.&nbsp; Car wash – we are asking Current (already received a few registrations)
&amp; Old Boys to please come forward to help with the car wash
<br>
<br>For more information or if you wish to contribute in any way, please contact
Fidelis Pang (affectionately known as Fido), SMSAA EXCO Member, at 9623
0455 or at&nbsp;<a href="mailto:oldboys.sjij.sms@gmail.com?subject=I%20will%20be%20coming%20to%20the%20Car%20Wash!&amp;litebox=1" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>oldboys.sjij.sms@gmail.com</u></a>.

<p><strong>AGM REPORT BY SMSAA PRESIDENT FOR THE YEAR 2016 – 2017</strong>
</p>
<p>Dear Members,</p>
<p>A year has passed so quickly and over that period, it has been extremely
heartening to see the strong and unwavering support of the old boys of
St Michael's School and SJI Junior. The Michaelian and Josephian spirit
is truly strong within us!</p>
<p>One particular example of this was when the school needed to fund the
construction of its new artificial turf pitch and improvements to various
parts of the school building.&nbsp; Members responded immediately and overwhelmingly
with contributions amounting to more than $10,000 within a short space
of time.&nbsp; The cost of the pitch was fully covered and the boys now
have a great facility to play on and also better conditions to study in.</p>
<p>On 21 May 2016, we organized a talk by Adrian Lim, a registered psychologist
and counsellor, to improve parenting skills. The parents who attended the
talk, “Challenges in Raising Sons &amp; Daughters Today,” found it useful
and relevant and even suggested that it be a regular feature of SMSAA’s
activities.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Last November, we also held our annual Thanksgiving “Mass &amp; Makan,”
that has now become a key feature in our calendar. It was a fantastic turnout
of old boys and their families. The SMSAA family that prays (and eats)
together will surely stay strong together!</p>
<p>The weekly indoor small-sided football sessions continue to take place,
as with the access to the school's sports facilities on selected days.&nbsp;
Also, we will continue to support sporting activities such as futsal and
badminton among alumni members and their families and friends. We invite
members to find out more by visiting the school's website.</p>
<p>Going forward, we see that food is one of the great tools for getting
members together, building camaraderie and stronger bonds among members.
Therefore, we are planning for more of such opportunities to achieve this
and to also strengthen the association with the school.</p>
<p>Other future activities that we are considering include a short trip to
nearby holiday destinations (for example, Kukup), talks for parents and
perhaps event excursions to places of interest (a walking heritage tour
of the old SJI by old boys!).</p>
<p>All these are platforms that we intend to adopt to connect and stay in
touch with each other.</p>
<p>As we plan for these activities, we hope to see more members at the various
gatherings and we welcome ideas and suggestions on ways to contribute back
to our alma mater.</p>
<p>God Bless.</p>
<p>Gerard Hooi</p>
<p>President, SMSAA</p>
<p>Saturday, 10th February 2017</p>

<h4>Letter from President</h4>
<p>2 May 2013</p>
<p>Dear Michaelians and Junior Josephians,</p>
<p>Welcome to the St Michael's School Alumni Association (SMSAA), formed
for fellowship and brotherhood. The School has offered a facility for old
boys to gather for sports and re union activities. The St Michael's Sports
Hall, after the school moves back to the Essex road location in June 2013,
will be available to members of the SMSAA who will be given priority (this
is being worked out with the Sports Council who manages the Hall). There
will also be a Heritage room which will house all forms of memorabilia,
to remind us of the halcyon days of the past. Sons of members of the SMSAA
also get higher priority when applying for admission to the school.</p>
<p>While we reminisce with our classmates and schoolmates, any assistance
to the school will be greatly appreciated. My classmates and I formed the
SMS Alumni fund to help needy students in SJI Junior, a few years ago.
We hope many more will come forward to assist the School, through this
SMSAA, in any way and activity that you can. The Pro- Tem committee will
plan a series of activities beginning with the re-union lunch on June 29,
2013, when SJI Junior moves back to its Essex road campus. The first Executive
Council (ExCo) will be elected into office in January, 2014, the 60th anniversary
of St. Michael's School. We look forward to many of you, who have the inclination,
to contribute your time and resources to serve in this Exco.</p>
<p>Please join the SMSAA as we remember our heritage and come together as
old friends.</p>
<p>Yours in St Michael,</p>
<p>Michael Ang</p>
<p>Class of 1966</p>
<p>President, Pro Tem committee</p>
<p>St Michael's School Alumni Association.</p>
